# INC-2025-3404 — Croissant Laminator CKE-LAM-2200-01, gauge-roller bearing wear

- **Date:** 2025-02-08
- **Line / Equipment:** CKE-Line-D / Croissant Laminator (Sheeting/Laminating)
- **Severity:** Medium
- **Product affected:** Butter Croissants

## Symptoms
Vibration RMS doubled from 1.3 to **2.7 mm/s** over two shifts. Power draw on
the gauge-roller drive crept up by 18 %. Defect rate climbed from 0.9 % to
**3.1 %** — dough sheet inconsistency producing uneven laminations and
collapsed proofing on a portion of each batch. Controller raised the seemingly
unrelated **E2009 — Freezer compressor cycling abnormally** before the operator
noticed the upstream sheeting issue.

## Root cause
Wear on the **gauge-roller drive-side bearing** (part **BRG-LAM-2200**)
introduced runout in the roller gap, varying sheet thickness and feeding
inconsistent dough to the freezer downstream (which is why the E2009 freezer
alarm appeared first — the unit was cycling to handle out-of-spec load).

## Corrective action
Replace **BRG-LAM-2200**, re-grease the roller housings, re-set the gauge gap
with a feeler gauge to spec, and run a 30-piece test batch to verify defect
rate ≤ 1.0 %.

## Impact
- **Downtime:** 2.7 hours
- **Cost:** $7,300
