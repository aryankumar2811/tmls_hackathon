# INC-2025-3402 — CIP System BRA-CIP-7700-01, electrical fault / arc flash risk

- **Date:** 2025-02-26
- **Line / Equipment:** SAN-Line-A / CIP System (Sanitation)
- **Severity:** Critical
- **Product affected:** Sanitation cycle for Donut line

## Symptoms
Power draw climbed from a nominal 4.0 kW to **22.7 kW** with intermittent spikes.
Motor temperature hit **96 °C**. Pressure across the recirculation loop dropped
from 105 PSI to **41 PSI** as the pump labored. Controller raised **E3009 —
Electrical fault — arc flash risk** during a routine caustic-rinse cycle.
Multiple sensors went offline simultaneously (`Multiple Sensors Offline`).

## Root cause
A damaged contactor on the recirculation pump's drive circuit caused arcing and
intermittent over-current draw. Insulation degradation propagated to the
adjacent sensor harness, which is why several sensors went offline together.

## Corrective action
LOTO the panel; replaced contactor **CTR-CIP-7700**, the affected sensor
harness, and the recirculation pump's motor windings. Megger-tested all
conductors. Re-commissioned the unit with a dry-cycle test before returning to
caustic rinse.

## Impact
- **Downtime:** 8.1 hours (sanitation pushed to next shift)
- **Cost:** $19,400
