# INC-2025-3401 — Robotic Decorator VAU-DEC-3300-01, drive-end bearing seizure

- **Date:** 2025-03-14
- **Line / Equipment:** PKG-Line-G / Robotic Decorator (Decorating)
- **Severity:** Critical
- **Product affected:** Cinnamon Rolls

## Symptoms
Vibration RMS climbed from 1.3 mm/s to 19 mm/s over a single shift. Motor
temperature reached **102 °C** and hydraulic fluid temperature **103 °C**. Noise
level peaked at **82 dB** — classic metal-on-metal acoustic signature. Decorator
positional precision collapsed: defect count went from baseline (~5) to **1,210
units in one batch** (6.9 % defect rate). Controller raised **E3002 —
Catastrophic bearing seizure**. Maintenance was 178 days overdue.

## Root cause
Drive-end bearing **BRG-DEC-3300** seized after lubricant film failure. The
collapsed film accelerated raceway wear, which drove the vibration spike, the
thermal cascade across the motor and hydraulic circuit, and the loss of
mechanical precision the decorator depends on.

## Corrective action
Emergency lockout. Replaced **BRG-DEC-3300** and the bearing housing seals,
flushed and refilled hydraulic fluid, re-greased the drive train with food-grade
NLGI-2, and ran the unit unloaded for 15 min to verify vibration ≤ 1.5 mm/s and
hydraulic fluid temp ≤ 55 °C.

## Impact
- **Downtime:** 6.4 hours
- **Cost:** $24,800 (parts + scrap)
