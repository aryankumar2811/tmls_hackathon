# SOP — Drive-end / Roller Bearing Replacement (generic)

**Scope:** Replacing degraded bearings on any rotating-machinery asset (robotic
decorator, croissant laminator, modular belt conveyor, spiral freezer drum,
etc.) once a vibration or temperature alert escalates to the failure-imminent
band.

**Trigger:** Vibration RMS above the equipment's E2002/E2008-class threshold,
bearing or motor temperature above the equipment's E3002-class threshold, or a
predictive-model class 3 (critical) classification.

## Safety
1. Lock out / tag out the drive and any associated hydraulic / pneumatic pack.
2. Bleed pressure to zero; verify zero energy at the cabinet with a meter.
3. Cool the equipment to ambient before disassembly.

## Procedure
1. Identify the affected bearing from the manual (part number, e.g.
   **BRG-DEC-3300**, **BRG-LAM-2200**, **BRG-CNV-1300-DE**, **BRG-FRZ-9100-DRV**).
2. Remove the housing cover; photograph wiring/seal orientation.
3. Pull the bearing with a bearing puller (avoid shock loading the shaft).
4. Press in the replacement; replace housing seals; re-grease per the equipment
   manual (food-grade NLGI-2 for ambient drives; low-temp grease for freezer
   drives).
5. Reassemble; restore lubrication, hydraulic fluid, or coolant levels.
6. Run unloaded 10–15 min; verify vibration RMS within the equipment's nominal
   band and temperatures within ± 10 % of baseline.

## Records
Log part number, technician, downtime, post-repair sensor readings, and the
triggering predictive-model class in the CMMS.

SOP, bearing replacement, lockout tagout, vibration RMS, motor temperature,
food-grade grease, low-temp grease, predictive maintenance class 3, BRG-*.
